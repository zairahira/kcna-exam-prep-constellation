## Why roles matter on the KCNA

The KCNA tests basic familiarity with the cloud native *organisational*
model, not just the technology. Expect questions about what a
"platform engineer" does vs. a "developer," and about concepts like
DevOps and Site Reliability Engineering.


## The common roles

### Developer / Application Engineer

Writes the application code. In a cloud native org they typically:

- Package the app as a container image
- Author the Kubernetes manifests for their service (Deployment,
  Service, ConfigMap, etc.)
- Own the service end-to-end including its observability and on-call

### Platform Engineer / SRE (Site Reliability Engineer)

Runs the platform that the developers deploy to. Typical responsibilities:

- Kubernetes cluster operations (upgrades, node pool management, capacity)
- Shared platform services (observability stack, service mesh, CI/CD
  infrastructure, secrets management)
- Reliability engineering: SLOs, incident response, postmortems
- Making the developer experience smooth - internal developer platforms
  (IDPs), paved paths, golden paths

The term **SRE** originated at Google and emphasises applying software
engineering to operations problems. The terms SRE, Platform Engineer,
Infrastructure Engineer, and DevOps Engineer overlap heavily in practice.

### Security Engineer

Focuses on the security posture:

- Policy (OPA Gatekeeper / Kyverno / admission rules)
- Vulnerability management (image scanning, patching)
- Identity (RBAC, workload identity, secrets management)
- Incident response for security events

### Architect

Sets technical direction:

- Evaluating and selecting technologies
- Designing cross-cutting concerns (service-to-service auth, data flow,
  tenancy)
- Reviewing significant designs


## How they interact: the DevOps lens

The old "devs throw code over the wall to ops" model is what cloud
native aims to replace. **DevOps** is less a job title and more a
cultural philosophy:

- **Shared responsibility** - developers care about operations;
  operators understand the apps they run.
- **Automation** - if it&apos;s manual and repeated, automate it.
- **Fast feedback loops** - CI/CD, observability, post-incident reviews.
- **Blameless postmortems** - treat incidents as learning opportunities.

"DevOps Engineer" as a job title usually refers to platform/SRE work in
practice.


## Platform engineering

The newest framing. Rather than "dev owns dev, ops owns ops," the idea
is:

- A **platform team** builds an internal product - the "paved path" for
  deploying and running apps at the organisation.
- **Developers** consume this platform as self-service. They still own
  their services, but the platform abstracts away Kubernetes YAML,
  CI/CD wiring, observability bootstrap, etc.

Tools here: **Backstage** (CNCF Incubating - developer portal),
**Crossplane** (CNCF Incubating - platform building blocks), custom
CRDs and operators that encode organisational conventions.

The shift: instead of every developer learning every detail of
Kubernetes, the platform team encodes the right defaults and developers
consume them.
